Lateral raises

Lateral raises are a shoulder-strengthening exercise. Stand with a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. While keeping a slight bend in your elbows, raise the dumbbells to the sides until they're at shoulder height. Lower them back down with control. Lateral raises target the lateral deltoid muscles, enhancing shoulder width and definition. Maintain proper posture, engage your core, and avoid swinging. Use manageable weights to ensure correct form. Incorporate lateral raises into your shoulder workout routine to improve shoulder strength and aesthetics. Beginners should start with lighter weights and gradually increase as strength and technique improve.

Rear delt pulls

Rear delt cable pulls, or cable face pulls, target the rear deltoid muscles. Attach a rope to a cable machine at chest height. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding the rope with an overhand grip. Pull the rope towards your face, squeezing your shoulder blades together, and focus on using your rear deltoids. Control the return to the starting position. This exercise enhances upper back and shoulder strength, improving posture and shoulder stability. Maintain proper form, avoid shrugging your shoulders, and use manageable weights.

Pike push ups

Pike push-ups are a bodyweight exercise that targets the shoulders, triceps, and core muscles. Start in a downward dog yoga pose with your hips raised, forming an inverted V shape. Lower your upper body by bending your arms, aiming your head toward the ground, and then press back up. Pike push-ups build upper body strength, shoulder stability, and flexibility. Keep your core engaged, maintain a straight line from head to hips, and ensure proper hand placement for wrist comfort. Modify the exercise by adjusting the angle of your body.

Shoulder press

The shoulder press, also known as the overhead press, is a weightlifting exercise that targets the shoulders, triceps, and upper back. Stand or sit, holding a barbell or dumbbells at shoulder level. Press the weight overhead by extending your arms, then lower it back down with control. The shoulder press enhances shoulder strength, muscle development, and upper body stability. Maintain proper posture, engage your core, and avoid excessive arching in the lower back. Start with manageable weights, focusing on controlled movement. Seek guidance, especially if new to the exercise, to ensure correct form and prevent strain or injury.
