Leg raises

Leg raises are a bodyweight exercise that targets the abdominal muscles and hip flexors. Lie flat on your back and keep your legs straight. Lift your legs off the ground by engaging your lower abs, then lower them back down without letting your feet touch the ground. Leg raises strengthen the core, improve lower abdominal definition, and enhance hip flexibility. Maintain a steady pace, avoid swinging, and focus on controlled movements. For added challenge, perform variations like hanging leg raises. Beginners should start with bent knee raises and progress gradually. Consult a fitness expert if needed to ensure proper form and prevent strain.

Plank

The plank is a simple yet effective core exercise. Begin in a push-up position, resting on your forearms instead of hands. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core, glutes, and shoulders. Hold this position for a set amount of time. Planks build core strength, stability, and endurance. They also engage muscles in the shoulders, back, and legs. Focus on maintaining proper alignment, avoiding sagging hips or raised buttocks. As you progress, increase the duration of the hold. Planks are versatile and can be modified for different fitness levels, making them a valuable core workout.

Hollow-body

The hollow body is an exercise that involves lying on your back while engaging your core muscles to create a flat, extended body position. Lift your legs and shoulders slightly off the ground, while keeping your lower back pressed into the floor. Your arms can also be extended overhead. The hollow body position builds core strength, enhances stability, and improves body awareness. It's commonly used in gymnastics and functional fitness training. Focus on maintaining a strong core engagement, a neutral spine, and a straight body line. Gradually increase the duration and intensity to challenge your core muscles effectively.
