Lunges

Lunges are a lower body exercise involving stepping forward or backward with one leg while bending both knees to create two 90-degree angles. The front knee should not extend past the ankle. Push back to the starting position and switch legs. Lunges target the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes while also engaging core muscles for stability. They enhance lower body strength, balance, and flexibility. Maintain an upright posture, avoid letting the knee go beyond the toes, and ensure both knees are aligned.

Squats

The squat is a fundamental lower body exercise. Begin with feet shoulder-width apart, lower your hips and bend your knees to lower your body, as if sitting back in a chair. Keep your chest up and your back straight. Lower until your thighs are parallel to the ground or deeper, then push through your heels to return to a standing position. Squats target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core. They build leg strength, improve functional movement, and enhance overall lower body power. Maintain proper form, avoid rounding your back, and start with body weight or light weights to ensure safety and effectiveness.

Hip-thrust

The hip thrust is a lower body strength exercise. Start by sitting on the ground with your upper back against a bench. Position a barbell over your hips and roll it onto your lap. Lower your upper body until your shoulder blades touch the bench. Plant your feet firmly on the ground and lift your hips toward the ceiling, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower your hips back down and repeat. Hip thrusts target the glutes and hamstrings, aiding in building lower body strength, enhancing hip power, and improving posture. Use proper form, focus on hip extension, and start with manageable weights.

Bulgarian split squat

The Bulgarian split squat is a lower body exercise that improves leg strength, balance, and flexibility. Stand a few feet in front of a bench or platform. Place one foot behind you on the bench, while the other foot stays forward. Lower your body by bending your front knee, aiming to create a 90-degree angle with both legs. Keep your torso upright and avoid letting your front knee go past your toes. Push through your front heel to return to the starting position. The exercise targets the quads, hamstrings, and glutes while enhancing stability and single-leg strength.
